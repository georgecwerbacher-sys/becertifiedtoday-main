KEYWORD DEMAND & ESTIMATED MONTHLY CLICKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Research date: 2026-06-11
Geo focus: United States (primary); Philippines (secondary reference)

IMPORTANT — two different numbers:
  • Monthly SEARCHES = how often people type the query on Google (market demand).
  • Monthly CLICKS = what your ads actually receive (only known from Google Ads
    after the campaign runs). Tables below use modeled estimates, not BCT history.

Method:
  1. Google Trends (US, trailing 12 months) for relative demand between terms.
  2. Anchor scaling: "ccnp encor" modeled at ~2,400 US searches/mo (typical
     GKP/SEO-tool band for this head term; cross-check in your Google Ads account).
  3. Long-tail terms with zero Trends signal treated as <10 US searches/mo.
  4. Estimated ad clicks assume $10/day budget, max CPC $2.75, phrase/exact mix,
     ~5–8% CTR when shown, and ~70–110 total campaign clicks/mo across ALL
     keywords (budget + low volume cap). Per-keyword clicks are shares of that
     total—not additive guarantees.

SERP / ads positioning takeaways
--------------------------------
• Head terms ("encor practice test", "ccnp encor") — Boson, Crucial Exams, and
  OpenExamPrep dominate organic; expect higher CPC and lower impression share.
• "Question bank" exact phrases — very low volume; cheap clicks but almost no
  scale. Keep for relevance, not volume.
• Best BCT angles in copy: 400+ browser questions, 8 lab simulations, v1.2,
  $19.99/30-day one-time (not subscription), free samples before checkout.
• Weak competitor gaps: few combine CLI labs + drag-and-drop + timed sim in one
  browser portal without GNS3 or a video-course subscription.
• Do not compete on dump/recall sites in ad copy; keep "dump" / "brain dump"
  negatives and Cisco-verified explanation messaging.

Suggested keyword priority (first 30 days at $10/day)

3. "encor 350-401 prep" — exam-code specificity
