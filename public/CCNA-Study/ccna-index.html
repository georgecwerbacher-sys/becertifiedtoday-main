CCNA study lines up with six main knowledge domains. Cisco publishes the current CCNA 200-301 v1.1 exam areas as network fundamentals, network access, IP connectivity, IP services, security fundamentals, and automation and programmability. The blueprint puts the strongest emphasis on IP connectivity, followed closely by fundamentals and access—exact percentages shift slightly over time, but those three fields carry most of what you will see on routing, switching, and troubleshooting-style items.

Approximate domain weights are taken from Cisco exam guidance—use them to budget study hours, not as a guarantee of item counts on your specific attempt.

Where to aim your reading and labs
Section Weight What to study
Network fundamentals 20% OSI and TCP/IP models, IPv4 and IPv6, cabling, devices, topologies, subnetting basics
Network access 20% VLANs, trunking, basic STP, EtherChannel, wireless concepts, switch port configuration
IP connectivity 25% Routing tables, static routing, OSPF fundamentals, path selection, troubleshooting routing
IP services 10% NAT, DHCP, DNS, NTP, SNMP, syslog, QoS basics
Security fundamentals 15% Device hardening, ACLs, authentication, AAA basics, secure management access
Automation and programmability 10% APIs, controllers, automation concepts, JSON, Python basics, intent-based networking

What to focus on first

Start with network fundamentals and IP connectivity: they underpin subnetting, routing, and a large share of drag-and-drop or simulation-style prompts. Then layer in network access and security fundamentals, which show up on both theory and configuration-style questions.

Study order suggestion

  1. Network fundamentals
  2. Subnetting and VLSM
  3. Switching and VLANs
  4. Routing and OSPF
  5. Services such as NAT, DHCP, and NTP
  6. Security and automation basics

For time management, lean hardest into the two weightiest pillars—IP connectivity and network fundamentals—before you polish lighter domains. That mirrors where most candidates lose or gain points under time pressure.
