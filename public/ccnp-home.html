Match your prep to how Cisco weights ENCOR 350-401

The live ENCOR 350-401 exam (v1.2) certifies professional-level enterprise networking skills across architecture, infrastructure, security, assurance, virtualization, and automation. Cisco publishes six domains; official guidance puts the strongest emphasis on Infrastructure (30%), followed by Security (20%) and Architecture (15%).

Domain weights below come from Cisco exam guidance. Use them to budget study hours, not as a guarantee of how many questions you will see on a specific attempt. The exam allows up to 120 minutes with multiple-choice, drag-and-drop, and simulation-style items.

Section Weight What to study
1.0 Architecture 15% Enterprise design, Catalyst SD-WAN, SD-Access, QoS, and high availability
2.0 Virtualization 10% Hypervisors, VMs, VRF, GRE, IPsec, LISP, and VXLAN
3.0 Infrastructure 30% Layer 2 trunking and STP, EtherChannel, EIGRP/OSPF, eBGP, NAT, FHRP, and multicast
4.0 Network Assurance 10% SNMP, syslog, NetFlow, SPAN/RSPAN, IPSLA, Catalyst Center, NETCONF, and RESTCONF
5.0 Security 20% Device access, AAA, ACLs, CoPP, REST API security, and security design
6.0 Automation and AI 15% Python, JSON, YANG, Catalyst Center and SD-WAN APIs, EEM, and orchestration tools

What to focus on first: infrastructure and architecture underpin routing, switching, and a large share of configuration-style prompts. Add security and network assurance next, then virtualization and automation for the full ENCOR mix.

Suggested study order

  1. Architecture and design principles
  2. Infrastructure: Layer 2, routing, and path selection
  3. Security: ACLs, CoPP, AAA, and device hardening
  4. Network assurance: monitoring, telemetry, and troubleshooting tools
  5. Virtualization: VRF, overlays, and tunneling
  6. Automation and AI: APIs, Python, JSON, and programmability

Under time pressure, leaning hardest into Infrastructure and Security before you polish lighter domains mirrors where Cisco weights the blueprint. The timed simulation on this site rehearses that mixed multiple-choice, drag-and-drop, and CLI lab pressure across all six domains in one exam-style session.

Source: Cisco ENCOR 350-401 exam blueprint, v1.2.
