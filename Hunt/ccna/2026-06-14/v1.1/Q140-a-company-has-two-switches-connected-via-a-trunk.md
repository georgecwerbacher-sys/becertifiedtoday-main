A company has two switches connected via a trunk link. Switch A has VLANs 10, 20, and 30 configured, but Switch B only has VLANs 10 and 20 in its VLAN database. What happens when a frame tagged with VLAN 30 arrives at Switch B from the trunk?

- A. Switch B automatically creates VLAN 30 and forwards the frame
- B. Switch B drops the frame because VLAN 30 does not exist in its VLAN database
- C. Switch B forwards the frame on all ports as an unknown unicast
- D. Switch B strips the VLAN 30 tag and forwards the frame on the native VLAN

**Stated answer (external):** B
